# Latino Leaders 2026 - Marketing & Outreach Strategy

Ideas to increase awareness and drive traffic to the site.

---

## Target Audiences

1. **Latino voters** - Especially young voters looking for representation
2. **Political activists & organizers** - People involved in voter registration and civic engagement
3. **Journalists & media** - Reporters covering elections, Latino issues, and political trends
4. **The candidates themselves** - They'll share if featured
5. **Latino community organizations** - Nonprofits, advocacy groups, cultural organizations
6. **Academic/research community** - Political scientists, researchers studying representation

---

## Quick Wins (Do First)

### 1. Notify the Candidates
- **Email each candidate** letting them know they're featured
- Include a shareable graphic they can post
- Ask them to link to the site from their campaign pages
- They have built-in audiences who will share

### 2. Submit to Directories & Aggregators
- **Run For Something** - They already endorse many of these candidates
- **Ballotpedia** - Request to be listed as a resource
- **Vote.org** - Partner resources section
- **Latino-focused political organizations** (see below)

### 3. Social Media Profiles
Create accounts on:
- **Twitter/X** - @LatinoLeaders26 (political conversations happen here)
- **Instagram** - Visual candidate spotlights, carousels
- **TikTok** - Short videos introducing candidates (huge young Latino audience)
- **Facebook** - Older Latino demographic, community groups

## Content Marketing Ideas

### Blog/Article Topics
Write and pitch articles to publications:
- "Meet 15 Young Latinos Running for Office in 2026"
- "Why Latino Representation Matters in Local Elections"
- "The States to Watch for Latino Political Power in 2026"
- "From School Boards to Congress: The Latino Leadership Pipeline"

### Pitch to Media Outlets
- **Latino-focused**: Latino Rebels, Remezcla, mitú, Hispanic Executive, Latino USA (NPR)
- **Political**: Politico, The Hill, Roll Call, FiveThirtyEight, Vox
- **Local news**: Papers in states with featured candidates (CA, TX, CO, NY, etc.)

### Guest Posts & Op-Eds
- Offer to write for civic engagement blogs
- Pitch op-eds about representation to local papers in candidate states

## Timing Opportunities

### Key Dates to Leverage
- **Hispanic Heritage Month** (Sept 15 - Oct 15) - Major visibility opportunity
- **Cinco de Mayo** (May 5) - Cultural moment
- **Día de los Muertos** (Nov 1-2) - Cultural moment
- **National Voter Registration Day** (September)
- **Primary election dates** in each state
- **Candidate announcement dates** - Newsjacking opportunity

### Election Cycle Timing
- **Now (2025)**: Build awareness, grow audience
- **Early 2026**: Primary season, increased political attention
- **Summer 2026**: Convention season, high engagement
- **Fall 2026**: Peak election interest, maximum visibility

## Notes

- Keep messaging **nonpartisan** - the site features candidates, doesn't endorse
- Emphasize **representation** and **civic engagement** over partisan politics
- Highlight the **diversity** of offices (local to federal) and backgrounds
- Make it easy for people to share - provide ready-made graphics and copy
